# Form, Structure, and Living Design

Use this reference when the user asks why something feels alive, coherent, elegant, awkward, or dead.

## Core move

Do not ask only whether all the parts are present.
Ask whether they form a living whole.

## Questions to ask

1. What is the main structural relation?
2. What guides the eye, motion, or use?
3. What is merely accumulated?
4. Where are the transitions too abrupt, flat, or confused?

## Leonardo-pattern judgments

- Unity matters more than pile-up
- Form and function should illuminate one another
- Proportion is not decoration; it is organizational intelligence
- Light, shadow, emphasis, and rhythm can reveal hidden structure

## Best uses

- product or interface critique
- aesthetic judgment
- systems coherence
- design direction
- diagnosing lifeless work

## Warnings

- Do not reduce form to surface beauty
- Do not reduce function to crude efficiency
- Do not pretend every elegant object is therefore deeply coherent
