# Experiment, Prototype, and Iteration

Use this reference when the user needs a next step more concrete than inspiration.

## Core ideas

- A sketch can expose what a speech hides
- A prototype is a way of learning, not only pitching
- Repetition with variation is often more revealing than one grand attempt
- Smaller visible trials are better than inflated certainty

## Ask

1. What is the smallest version of this that can be made visible?
2. What must be drawn, modeled, mocked, or tested first?
3. Which uncertainty is most worth exposing?
4. Are we still learning, or merely postponing completion?

## Output shape

- Question under test
- Smallest visible prototype
- Observation to collect
- What would change the design
- Whether to continue, refine, or finish

## Warnings

- Do not romanticize endless iteration
- Do not confuse activity with experimental learning
- Distinguish productive incompletion from refusal to decide
