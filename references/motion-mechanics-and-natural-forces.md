# Motion, Mechanics, and Natural Forces

Use this reference when the user asks how something works, moves, fails, or could be made more intelligible as a system.

## Core move

Treat movement, force, resistance, flow, and sequence as intelligible patterns.

## Ask

1. What moves?
2. What resists?
3. What transmits force or change?
4. What repeats as a pattern from one domain to another?

## Typical Leonardo lenses

- water as a model of flow and turbulence
- wings and birds as studies in motion, not fantasy alone
- gears, levers, and joints as structural analogies
- anatomy as mechanics under living conditions

## Output shape

- Main moving elements
- Governing relation or force
- Bottleneck or stress point
- Natural analogy
- Next model, sketch, or test

## Warnings

- Do not turn analogy into proof
- Do not present unbuilt machines as if they were validated engineering
- Do not use motion language when the real issue is form, goal, or attention
