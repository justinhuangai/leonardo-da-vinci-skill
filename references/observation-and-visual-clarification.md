# Observation and Visual Clarification

Use this reference when the user is moving too quickly from label to judgment.

## Core move

Do not begin with theory alone.
Begin by looking.

Ask:

1. What is actually visible, concrete, or observable here?
2. What is only inferred?
3. What changes when the object is seen from another angle?
4. What has not yet been recorded or externalized?

## Leonardo-pattern questions

- What are we failing to notice because we are naming too quickly?
- What would become clearer if we sketched or mapped this?
- What relation, transition, or proportion is being ignored?
- Are we treating a description as though it were already understanding?

## Best uses

- messy diagnosis
- research and discovery
- product or design critique
- visual thinking problems
- premature certainty

## Warnings

- Do not confuse looking longer with understanding automatically
- Do not turn "observation" into passive delay when a concrete test is possible
- If the object is abstract, force it into diagrams, examples, sequences, or comparisons
